# Common Mistakes in English (A1–C2)

## A1 Common Mistakes
### Verb to be
- Wrong: I is happy.
- Correct: I am happy.

### Present Simple
- Wrong: She play football.
- Correct: She plays football.

### Articles
- Wrong: I have apple.
- Correct: I have an apple.

### Possessive Adjectives
- Wrong: This is she book.
- Correct: This is her book.

---

## A2 Common Mistakes
### Past Simple
- Wrong: He goed to school.
- Correct: He went to school.

### Present Continuous
- Wrong: I am go to school.
- Correct: I am going to school.

### Comparatives and Superlatives
- Wrong: She is more smarter than me.
- Correct: She is smarter than me.

### Countable / Uncountable Nouns
- Wrong: I have some breads.
- Correct: I have some bread.

---

## B1 Common Mistakes
### Present Perfect vs Past Simple
- Wrong: I have eaten yesterday.
- Correct: I ate yesterday.

### Conditionals
- Wrong: If I will go, I would see him.
- Correct: If I went, I would see him.

### Relative Clauses
- Wrong: The man which called me is nice.
- Correct: The man who called me is nice.

### Passive Voice
- Wrong: The letter send by me.
- Correct: The letter was sent by me.

---

## B2 Common Mistakes
### Mixed Tenses in Narrative
- Wrong: I was eating when he has arrived.
- Correct: I was eating when he arrived.

### Reported Speech
- Wrong: He said me he was tired.
- Correct: He told me he was tired.

### Third Conditional
- Wrong: If I had studied, I would pass.
- Correct: If I had studied, I would have passed.

---

## C1 Common Mistakes
### Advanced Conditionals
- Wrong: If I will have known, I would help.
- Correct: Had I known, I would have helped.

### Future Perfect / Continuous
- Wrong: By tomorrow I will finish my work.
- Correct: By tomorrow I will have finished my work.

### Cleft Sentences
- Wrong: It John who solved the problem.
- Correct: It was John who solved the problem.

---

## C2 Common Mistakes
### Precision in Modals and Aspect
- Wrong: He must went there.
- Correct: He must have gone there.

### Nominalization
- Wrong: He made a decide.
- Correct: He made a decision.

### Discourse Markers
- Wrong: Nevertheless, he don’t like it.
- Correct: Nevertheless, he does not like it.
